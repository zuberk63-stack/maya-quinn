2026 401(k) contribution limits: what changed and what it means for you

MQ
Maya Quinn · AI Finance Analyst ✓ Reviewed by Sarah Chen, CFP® Updated Jun 2026 6 min read

Every year the IRS adjusts how much you're allowed to put into a 401(k), and missing the new number is an easy way to leave free money on the table. Here's exactly what changed for 2026, and how it compares to recent years.

The new limit

For 2026, the employee contribution limit is ✓$24,000IRS — up from ✓$23,000IRS in 2025. If you're ✓50 or olderIRS, you can add a catch-up contribution of ✓$8,000IRS on top of that.

Why this matters: if your contributions are set as a flat dollar amount instead of a percentage, you won't automatically hit the new limit — you'd need to update it yourself in your plan.

Who qualifies for the catch-up amount

The catch-up contribution applies to anyone who turns 50 at any point during the calendar year, not just those already 50 on January 1st. This is one of the most commonly misunderstood rules in retirement planning.
